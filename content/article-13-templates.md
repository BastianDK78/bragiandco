# EU AI Act — Article 13 Notice Templates

**Version:** 1.0  
**Updated:** 2026-05-12  
**Purpose:** Ready-to-use disclosure templates for deployers of Annex III §4 employment AI. Article 13 of the EU AI Act requires that persons affected by AI-assisted decisions are informed that AI is being used. These templates satisfy that obligation.

> **Disclaimer:** These are template starting points, not legal advice. Your legal team should review before issuing. Requirements may vary based on your national implementation, employment law, and the specific AI systems deployed.

---

## How to use these templates

1. Identify which AI systems you have deployed from the heatmap (Red and Yellow §4 rows)
2. Select the appropriate template (applicant-facing or employee-facing)
3. Fill in all [bracketed] fields with your specific details
4. Have your legal team review before issuing
5. Deliver via a channel that creates a record: email confirmation, signed acknowledgment in onboarding paperwork, or documented notice in your ATS/HRIS

**One template per system is not required.** If you use multiple AI systems in hiring, you may issue a single notice that names all of them. The requirement is that the person knows which systems are in use and what they analyze — not that each system has its own notice.

**GDPR privacy notice is not sufficient.** Article 13 requires a specific disclosure about the AI system in use and the nature of decisions it influences. A reference to "automated processing" in a GDPR notice does not discharge this obligation.

## Template 1 — Job Applicant AI Disclosure Notice

**When to use:** Any time an AI system is used to screen, score, rank, or filter job applications, or to assess candidates in structured interviews or video assessments.

**Relevant heatmap rows:** Workday Recruiting Agent (Red), HireVue AI-Scored Video Interviews (Yellow), HireVue Game-Based Assessments (Yellow), Greenhouse Talent Rediscovery (Yellow), Greenhouse Resume Review (Yellow), Oracle HCM Job Applicant Score (Yellow), SAP SuccessFactors Candidate Matching (Yellow).

---

**Subject line (if delivered by email):** Notice of AI-assisted tools used in our recruitment process

---

Dear [Applicant name / Dear Candidate],

Thank you for applying for the [Job Title] position at [Company Name].

**AI-assisted tools in our recruitment process**

As part of our application review process, [Company Name] uses AI-assisted tools to support our recruitment decisions. In the interest of transparency, we want to inform you of the specific tools in use and how they are applied.

**Tool(s) in use:**

[Select and complete the applicable rows below. Delete rows that do not apply.]

| Tool | What it analyses | What it produces |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| [Workday Recruiting Agent] | Your CV content, work history, and application responses against the requirements of the role | A match score and ranked shortlist recommendation |
| [HireVue AI-Scored Video Interview] | Your verbal responses, response structure, and job-relevant competencies during the recorded interview | A competency assessment score per interview dimension |
| [HireVue Game-Based Assessments] | Cognitive and behavioural patterns from structured game tasks | An assessment of cognitive traits relevant to the role |
| [Greenhouse Talent Rediscovery] | Your prior application history in our candidate database | A relevance ranking for open roles |
| [Oracle HCM Job Applicant Score] | Your CV and application responses against role requirements | A fit score used in initial screening |
| [SAP SuccessFactors Candidate Matching] | Your profile against role requirements and historical hiring patterns | A job-candidate fit score |

**How AI outputs are used**

AI outputs do not automatically advance or reject applications. A human reviewer considers the AI assessment alongside other information before any decision about your application is made.

**Your rights**

You have the right to:

- Request human review of any AI-assisted assessment that has influenced a decision about your application
- Ask us to explain how the AI assessment was used in your application review
- Contest an AI assessment you believe does not accurately reflect your qualifications

To exercise any of these rights, contact [HR contact name or team] at [email address]. We will respond within [timeframe, e.g., 5 business days].

**Data protection**

Your personal data is processed in accordance with our Privacy Notice, available at [URL]. AI-generated assessments of your application are [retained for X months / deleted after X days]. If you have questions about how your data is handled, contact our Data Protection Officer at [DPA contact].

[Company Name]  
[Date]

## Template 2 — Employee AI Disclosure Notice

**When to use:** When AI systems are used to generate performance insights, review summaries, or workforce analytics that may influence decisions about employees (performance ratings, compensation, role assignment, coaching, disciplinary processes).

[Company Name]

[Date]

## Template 3 — Contact Centre Customer AI Disclosure (§1(c))

**When to use:** When NICE CXone Real-Time Voice Emotion Detection or Facial Expression Analysis is active on customer interactions.

**Relevant heatmap rows:** NICE CXone Real-Time Voice Emotion Detection (Red), NICE CXone Facial Expression Analysis (Red).

**Note:** This is a §1(c) biometric obligation, not a §4 employment obligation. The affected persons are customers, not employees. Standard call recording disclosures do not cover AI emotion inference — this must be explicitly stated.

---

**Standard call opening script (voice channel):**

"This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes. We also use AI tools that analyse voice interactions to help us improve our service. By continuing with this call, you consent to this analysis. If you have any questions about how your data is used, please ask the agent or visit [privacy notice URL]."

---

**Chat / video channel disclosure (add to pre-chat consent screen):**

"Before we begin: [Company Name] uses AI-assisted tools during this interaction, including analysis of your voice [and/or video] to help improve our service quality. This analysis may include assessment of conversational characteristics. For full details, see our Privacy Notice at [URL]. To continue without AI voice/video analysis, please [state the alternative, e.g., use our text chat option / request a non-AI-assisted interaction]."

---

**Compliance note:** "Calls may be recorded" does not satisfy Article 13 for AI emotion detection. The disclosure must specifically state that AI is analysing voice or video characteristics. Update your standard call recording notice to include the AI analysis statement before August 2, 2026.

## Article 13 compliance reference

| Layer | Who is obliged | What is required |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| Art. 13(1) — Instructions for use | Vendor (as Provider) | Must provide deployer with adequate documentation of what the AI does, its capabilities, and limitations |
| Art. 13(2) — Informing users | You (as Deployer) | Must inform the persons subject to AI-assisted decisions that AI is being used; must describe the system and the nature of decisions it influences |
| Art. 13(3) — Transparency for high-risk AI | Vendor (as Provider) | System must be designed to allow deployers to comply with their transparency obligations — i.e., vendor must make it possible for you to explain what the AI does |

**The templates above address Art. 13(2) — your obligation.** The vendor's obligation under Art. 13(1) is discharged by their product documentation (AI Fact Sheets, DPA, instructions for use). If the vendor has not provided adequate instructions, that is a vendor gap — flag it using the checklist in `PROVIDER-DEPLOYER-MATRIX.md`.
