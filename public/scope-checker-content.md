# EU AI Act Scope Checker

**Are your enterprise AI systems in scope of the EU AI Act?**  
**Enforcement date: August 2, 2026**

This checklist takes under 5 minutes. Answer each question honestly. At the end, your score tells you where you stand and what to do next.

---

## The 10 questions

Mark each answer: Yes / No / Not sure

---

**1. Does your company have employees based in the EU?**

Even a single EU-based employee puts some AI obligations in scope. The EU AI Act applies to AI systems that affect EU residents — not just to EU-headquartered companies.

**2. Do you hire for EU-based roles?**

Job applicants are covered persons under the EU AI Act. If your recruiting process touches EU applicants — including those screened remotely by AI — your hiring AI is in scope.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**3. Do you use AI-assisted tools in your hiring or recruitment process?**

This includes CV screening, candidate ranking, structured interview scoring, video interview analysis, or any tool that generates a score or recommendation about a job applicant.

Examples: Workday Recruiting Agent, HireVue, Greenhouse Talent Rediscovery, Oracle Job Applicant Score, SAP SuccessFactors Candidate Matching.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**4. Do you use AI tools that assess or monitor employee performance, behaviour, or work patterns?**

This includes AI-generated performance review summaries, workforce analytics that score or rank employees, contact centre agent quality scoring, or any tool that produces AI assessments used in employment decisions.

Examples: Lattice AI, SAP SuccessFactors Joule, Microsoft Viva Insights, NICE CXone CSAT Add-On, Workday Talent Mobility Agent.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**5. Do you use AI in customer interactions involving voice or video analysis?**

Emotion detection, sentiment analysis, and facial expression analysis applied to customer calls or video interactions is a separate Annex III category (§1(c) biometrics). It has the same August 2, 2026 enforcement date.

Examples: NICE CXone Real-Time Voice Emotion Detection, Genesys Emotion AI.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**6. Have you confirmed which AI features are actually enabled in your systems?**

Most enterprises know what they purchased. Few know which AI features are active in their current configuration. Vendors enable AI capabilities by default or via routine updates — often without explicit operator action.

If you have not specifically checked your admin settings for the AI features listed in questions 3-5, mark this "No."

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**7. Do you have a written record of which AI systems you have deployed for EU employees or applicants?**

Article 9 requires a risk management system — a documented record naming each AI system, your deployment context, and your assessment of the risks of running it. A spreadsheet is sufficient. The absence of any written record is not.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**8. Have your employees and job applicants received a written notice that AI is used in decisions about them?**

Article 13 requires that affected persons are specifically informed. A GDPR privacy notice that mentions "automated processing" is not sufficient. The notice must name the system and describe what it analyses.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**9. Can you describe, in writing, how your team reviews and overrides AI recommendations before employment decisions are made?**

Article 14 requires meaningful human oversight — a human who can understand the AI output, question it, and override it with no penalty. "A human reviews everything" is not sufficient if the process is not documented and the override mechanism is not genuine.

[ ] Yes   [ ] No   [ ] Not sure

---

**10. Have you requested and received EU AI Act compliance documentation from your AI vendors?**

Your vendors (as providers) are responsible for technical documentation, conformity assessments, and EU AI Office registration. You (as deployer) need to confirm they are meeting those obligations. If you have not asked, mark this "No."
